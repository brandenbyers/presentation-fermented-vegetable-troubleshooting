Fear of Botulism

What is Botulism?

  • Caused by the Botulinum neurotoxin
    • 6 million times more toxic than rattle snake venom
    • Most deadly toxin known to humans
    • Equivalent of 1 grain of sand enough to kill an adult human
    • 1 kilogram is enough to kill the entire human population
  • Causes muscle weakness
    • Difficulty swallowing, blurred vision, trouble breathing
    • paralysis and death

Clostridium botulinum â€” it's everywhere!

  • Soil contact = C. botulinum spores
  • Inactive spores are like seeds
  • Spores produce active cells in ideal conditions

Fermented Vegetables are Botulism Bullies

Why can't C. botulinum succeed?

  • Salinity
    • High salt slows down C. botulinum
    • LAB love salt!
    • LAB outcompete C. botulinum
    • LAB lower pH
  • Acidity
    • C. botulinum can't survive below 4.6 ph

Real Danger: Improper Home Canning

  • C. botulinum spores withstand high heat
  • Heat wipes out competing bacteria
  • Low-acid foods are C. botulinum playgrounds

Troubleshooting

#1 Mistake: Non-Anaerobic Environment

Keep Your Vegetables Below the Brine

  • Finger method
  • Weights
    • Glass weights
    • Clay weights
    • Rocks
  • Jar-inside-jar
    • Covered with cloth
    • Closed inside jar lid
  • Plastic bag

Mold

How to Avoid Mold

  • Scrape it off
  • Keep All Vegetables Below Brine
  • Agitate Jar Daily
  • Maintain Anaerobic Environment

Yeast

Signs of Yeast

  • Yeasty Smell
  • Kahm Yeast

How to Avoid Yeast

  • Maintain anaerobic environment
  • Keep vegetables submerged

Discoloration

  • Pink
    • Too much salt
    • Improper salt distribution
    • Possible yeast infection
  • Brown
    • Oxidization
    • Most likely a sign of vitamin C depletion

Slimy Texture

  • Too little salt
  • Improper salt distribution
  • Fresh Herbs

Soft or Mushy

  • Salt
  • Temperature
  • Cucumber Pickles
